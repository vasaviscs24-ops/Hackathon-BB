UTSAV

BMSCE Annual Techno-Cultural Fest

The Legacy

UTSAV is the Annual Techno-Cultural fest hosted by B.M.S. College of Engineering in Bengaluru. Standing tall since 1981, it is the pride of BMSCE and one of the largest fests in South India. Over the years, UTSAV has established itself as a brand, providing an invaluable platform to explore one's talents and artistic endeavors.

Passionate students are given ample opportunities to express themselves and compete with one another, with creativity, courage, and persistence being rewarded generously. The festival has set new benchmarks with its events, making it an unforgettable experience.

Celebrating Heritage

Admired for its dedication to the rhythms of cosmopolitan Bengaluru. A flourishing hub of cultural excellence since 1981.

Edition 2023

UTSAV-REGALIA 2023

Fondly known as the Festival of Faith, Utsav 2023 spotlit the indomitable human spirit. The theme was ‘Regalia’ – emulating royalty; not just an emotion, but a way of life that makes one feel a sense of belonging in an exalted state of being.

Surpassing all expectations, it featured over 70 events spanning dance, music, theater, art, and gaming, supported by over 60 sponsors and collaborators.

ZeroWaste Fest

Green Fest Initiative

A zero-waste festival initiative using steel cutlery, awareness campaigns, and strategic waste segregation.

  • Usage of steel cutlery (plates, spoons, glasses) for all meals.
  • Strategic waste bins for bagasse, wood, and dry/wet waste.
  • Educational awareness before DJ night and Turtle Jam.

Edition 2022

UTSAV-ENVISAGE 2022

This year witnessed the amalgamation of UTSAV with ENVISAGE, the Annual Cultural Fest of B.M.S. College of Architecture. It was a fest of massive magnitude that pulled in crowds from across the nation.

UTSAV-ENVISAGE created a congenial atmosphere for the youth to channel their spirits and innovation, providing a platform for talented students in theater, dance, music, and debate to express themselves in the most innovative ways ever conceived.
